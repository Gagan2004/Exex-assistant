19 - Personal AI-Powered Executive Assistant PRD
Program: makeitnow.ai Productized Services PRD
Date: June 3, 2026 | Use: Internal developer handbook and PRD | Status: Draft v1
Service type
Personal operating system and high-trust assistant
One-line purpose
Help a single operator manage priorities, communication, scheduling, and follow-through with AI-assisted support.
Ideal audience
Founders, operators, and executives who need an assistant-like workflow system but do not want to rely on a fully manual EA.
Primary problem
Important tasks, messages, meetings, and follow-ups get scattered across tools and memory, creating avoidable friction and missed decisions.

End Goal Expectations
Make personal operations more organized, predictable, and low-friction.
Reduce repetitive manual coordination work.
Turn recurring management tasks into repeatable AI-assisted systems.
Detailed Requirements
Client inputs
The operator must identify the highest-value personal EA workflows before automation starts.
The operator must define which tasks can be handled automatically and which require review.
Sensitive actions, approvals, and communication boundaries must be explicitly defined.
At least one workflow should produce a measurable result after launch.
Delivery requirements
The personal operating workflow should be mapped before automation starts.
The system must save time, reduce missed actions, or improve clarity in a visible way.
The assistant should be able to draft, organize, summarize, or prioritize with minimal correction.
Deliverables
Workflow inventory with priority ranking.
Prototype implementation of the first high-value workflows.
Prompt, instruction, or agent configuration notes.
Failure log and human-review boundaries.
Recommendation for the best first version of the personal AI EA.
Version 2 roadmap.
Acceptance Criteria
At least three real EA workflows are tested.
Each tested workflow has a clear output and assessment.
The output clearly separates reliable automation, partial automation, and human-required steps.
The final recommendation is specific enough to turn into a build plan.
Success Metrics
The operator can restate the problem, scope, and next decision without extra clarification.
The output is specific enough to build, validate, or price without rewriting the brief.
The experiment produces a measurable productivity gain or a production-ready workflow asset.
The work leaves behind reusable prompts, workflow templates, or operating rules where applicable.
Typical Package Ladder
Diagnostic: 5 days, $3,500.
Prototype: 14 days, $9,500.
Managed: $3,500 per month.
Pricing and Profitability
The ladder prices are starting fixed-scope floors, not discount targets.
Standard packages should target roughly 65 to 75 percent gross margin before custom work or exceptions.
Anything involving sensitive data, broad permissions, cross-tool coordination, or high-trust communication should move up a tier or become a custom quote.
Use milestone billing when scope is longer than a short sprint: 50/25/25 for short projects and 40/30/30 for larger builds.
Every engagement should create reusable IP, templates, or playbooks; if not, the scope or price should be revised.
If the work depends on broad handholding or open-ended changes, narrow the scope before agreeing to the price.
Rare-Case Pricing Triggers
Access to sensitive inboxes or calendars.
Multi-tool synchronization.
Complex approval paths.
High-trust delegation or executive confidentiality requirements.
Best Follow-On Services
Calendar and inbox automation
Meeting prep and follow-up systems
Voice-to-task capture workflows
Agentic workflow architecture
Internal Notes
This is a high-leverage personal operating layer, not a generic chatbot.
The best assistant is the one that removes friction without creating new supervision overhead.
End state: a personal AI assistant system that can be trusted for real daily management work without creating hidden operational risk.
